# Specific Vehicle Problem Investigation Prompt 2
A targeted automotive failure-analysis prompt for researching a vehicle you already have in mind. It investigates major mechanical problems, electrical issues, repair costs, recalls, longevity, and mechanic opinions to determine whether a specific year, make, model, and engine is a safe buy, caution buy, or avoid.

A focused automotive research prompt for buyers who already have a specific vehicle in mind and want a mechanic-style failure analysis before purchasing.

Instead of asking broad questions about whether a model is “good,” this prompt investigates a particular year, make, model, and engine to uncover the real-world problems that matter most in ownership. It is designed to pull together reliability history, recurring failures, repair costs, recall patterns, and mechanic opinions so buyers can make smarter decisions before committing to a vehicle.

This is especially useful when a vehicle looks clean, has a reasonable price, and appears attractive on the surface — but may hide expensive engine, transmission, electronics, or cooling system problems that show up later.

What it does

Researches a specific year, make, model, and engine

Investigates engine failures, transmission problems, cooling system issues, and drivetrain weaknesses

Reviews electrical and software failures, including infotainment issues, digital display failures, sensor faults, and no-start conditions

Summarizes typical repair costs and the mileage ranges when major failures commonly appear

Checks recalls, safety issues, and technical service bulletins

Assesses expected longevity and lifespan

Gathers mechanic opinion and real-world ownership experience

Identifies the best years, worst years to avoid, and most reliable engine options

Produces a clear buying recommendation based on long-term risk

Best for

Used car buyers with a specific listing in mind

Private-party purchase research

Dealer lot due diligence

Families shopping for dependable transportation

Buyers comparing trims, years, or engine options

Anyone trying to avoid expensive used-car mistakes

Example use cases

“Investigate the reliability history of a 2016 Ford Escape 1.6L.”

“Check whether a 2015 Nissan Altima is a safe used-car purchase.”

“Review major problems and common repair costs for a 2017 Hyundai Sonata 2.4L.”

“Identify the best and worst years for the Chevrolet Equinox and which engine is safest to buy.”
